What It Measures

How close the input is to being a real business concept. 0.0 = definitively not a business. 1.0 = definitively a real business.

Why This Design

Scores are hand-calibrated to reflect severity ordering: physically impossible ideas (0.02) are worse than vague ones (0.35). This is a gate, not a model вҖ” scores are ordinal labels that rank rejection severity. The LLM arbiter's score is 1 вҲ’ confidence because higher LLM confidence in rejection means lower validity. Pass-through is 0.92 (not 1.0) because passing L0 doesn't guarantee viability.

Why It's Correct

A gate needs correct ordering (impossible < no revenue < vague < valid), not statistical calibration. The scores enforce that ordering by construction.

Layer 1 вҖ” Parser2. Aggregate Confidence GATE

Formula

aggregate_confidence = mean( confidence[f] for f in required_fields ) where required_fields = {business_model, target_segment, stage, differentiation_score} confidence[f] вҲҲ [0.0, 1.0] вҖ” per-field extraction confidence from LLM Gate: halts pipeline if aggregate_confidence < 0.50 Field becomes UNKNOWN if its individual confidence < 0.55

What It Measures

A sufficiency gate ensuring the LLM extracted the required fields with enough certainty. Below threshold вҶ’ pipeline asks for clarification instead of guessing.

Why This Design

Arithmetic mean treats all required fields equally. We gate on the mean rather than any single field because one low-confidence field shouldn't block if the others are strong вҖ” L2вҖ“L4 can compensate for one uncertain input but not for systemic ambiguity.

Why It's Correct

The mean penalizes evenly when multiple fields are weak, but allows one borderline field through when the rest are high. This matches the design intent: graceful degradation for one uncertain field, hard stop for widespread uncertainty.

Layer 2A вҖ” Macro Vector3. Effective Inflation SIGNAL

Formula

scale = base_inflation / 33.9 effective_inflation = clip( eff_inf_offset Г— scale, 1.0, 100.0 ) where: base_inflation = country-level inflation rate (COUNTRY_MACRO_DEFAULTS) eff_inf_offset = sector-specific inflation sensitivity (SECTOR_EFF_MACRO) 33.9 = Egypt's inflation rate (reference denominator)

What It Measures

Converts raw country inflation into a sector-adjusted effective inflation reflecting how much inflation actually impacts this particular sector.

Why This Design

Fintech (offset 7.5) experiences less inflation pass-through than ecommerce (offset 36.0) because digital services have lower COGS exposure. Dividing by 33.9 normalizes so the SVM sees values trained on Egypt-calibrated data regardless of country. Clipping prevents degenerate inputs.

Why It's Correct

Proportional rescaling: new = reference Г— (country / reference_country). Preserves relative ordering of sectors across countries while keeping magnitude in the SVM's training range.

Layer 2A вҖ” Macro Vector4. Macro Friction SIGNAL

Formula

macro_friction = clip( effective_inflation + unemployment вҲ’ gdp_growth, вҲ’50, 100 )

What It Measures

A single scalar capturing how much the macro environment resists startup creation. Higher friction = harder to operate.

Why This Design
  • Inflation adds friction вҖ” higher costs erode margins
  • Unemployment adds friction вҖ” weaker consumer spending, harder hiring
  • GDP growth reduces friction вҖ” expanding economy creates demand

The subtraction makes it a net resistance score: a growing economy partially offsets inflation and unemployment drag.

Why It's Correct

A signed linear combination where each component has clear directional interpretation. It's a proxy designed to separate favorable from hostile environments вҖ” exactly what the SVM needs as a feature input.

Layer 2D вҖ” Time Series5. SARIMA Trend Normalization SIGNAL

Formula

forecast_mean = mean( max(0, v) for v in sarima_forecast_values ) sarima_trend = clip( forecast_mean / 50.0, 0.15, 0.90 ) Default = 0.50 (neutral) when no SARIMA model exists for the sector

What It Measures

Normalized sector trajectory in [0.15, 0.90]. Higher = sector is growing, lower = sector is declining.

Why This Design
  • max(0, v) floors negative forecasts вҖ” negative deal counts are nonsensical
  • Dividing by 50.0 maps forecasts to 0вҖ“1 range (50 deals/period = neutral midpoint)
  • Clip to [0.15, 0.90] prevents extremes: no sector should contribute zero or dominating weight
Why It's Correct

Min-max normalization with a domain-informed midpoint. The divisor of 50 was calibrated from training data. Asymmetric clip ensures no signal saturation in either direction.

Layer 2C.5 вҖ” Implicit RAG8. SHAP Cosine Similarity SIGNAL

Formula

query_vec = [shap_shareвӮҒ, shap_shareвӮӮ, ..., shap_shareвӮ…] (current input) mean_vec = cluster_mean_shap[top_cluster] (training mean) query_vec В· mean_vec shap_cosine = clip( вҖ”вҖ”вҖ”вҖ”вҖ”вҖ”вҖ”вҖ”вҖ”вҖ”вҖ”вҖ”вҖ”вҖ”вҖ”вҖ”вҖ”вҖ”вҖ”вҖ”вҖ”вҖ”вҖ”вҖ” , 0.0, 1.0 ) вҖ–query_vecвҖ– Г— вҖ–mean_vecвҖ– Default = 0.5 when artifact unavailable or zero-norm vector

What It Measures

Attribution consistency: is the model reasoning about this case the same way it learned to reason about typical cases in this cluster?

  • 1.0 вҶ’ attributions identical to cluster mean (typical)
  • 0.5 вҶ’ neutral default (unavailable data)
  • 0.0 вҶ’ attributions orthogonal to cluster mean (atypical)
Why This Design

Cosine similarity is direction-only, ignoring magnitude вҖ” we compare the pattern of feature importance, not scale. Clip to [0,1] because negative cosine is meaningless here. Default 0.5 (not 0.0) prevents unfair penalization when artifact is missing.

Why It's Correct

Cosine similarity between two vectors measures the angle between them, normalized to [вҲ’1,1]. Clipping to [0,1] converts to a similarity degree. This is the standard method for measuring attribution pattern consistency in XAI literature.

RAG вҖ” Retrieval9. Novelty Score ROUTING

Formula

query_vec = L2_normalize( [x_scaled (5D), shap_shares (5D)] ) вҶ’ 10D vector max_cosine = max( cosine_similarity(query_vec, neighbor) for neighbor in kNN ) novelty_score = clip( 1.0 вҲ’ max_cosine, 0.0, 1.0 ) is_novel = (novelty_score > 0.40) вҶ’ RAG suppressed for novel cases

What It Measures

How far the current input is from any training point in the combined macro + attribution space.

  • 0.0 вҶ’ identical to a training point (known territory)
  • 1.0 вҶ’ completely orthogonal to all training points (fully novel)
Why This Design

Combined 10D vector captures both what the input looks like (macro) and how the model reasons about it (SHAP). L2-normalization converts FAISS inner-product search into cosine. 1 вҲ’ max_cosine because the closest neighbor's similarity is the best evidence of precedent.

Why It's Correct

Cosine distance = 1 вҲ’ cosine similarity is a standard metric. Threshold 0.40 means even the most similar training point shares <60% directional similarity вҖ” a conservative threshold catching genuinely out-of-distribution cases. RAG is suppressed because votes from distant neighbors produce fake confidence, not real evidence.

RAG вҖ” Retrieval10. RAG Confidence + ARIMA Modifier ROUTING

Formula

raw_confidence = votes_for_winner / k (majority vote fraction, k=5) ARIMA modifier: if sarima_trend вүҘ 0.65: confidence = min(1.0, raw_confidence + 0.10) elif sarima_trend вүӨ 0.35: confidence = raw_confidence Г— 0.70 else: confidence = raw_confidence

What It Measures

Confidence in the k-NN majority vote regime label, modulated by sector trend direction. Improving sectors make historical precedents more predictive; declining sectors make them less reliable.

Why This Design
  • Amplification (+0.10 additive): improving sector вҶ’ small confidence bonus
  • Dampening (Г—0.70 multiplicative): declining sector вҶ’ proportional penalty
  • Asymmetry is intentional: downside uncertainty is more dangerous than upside certainty is valuable
Why It's Correct

Additive boosts prevent weak-evidence amplification (0.40вҶ’0.50, not Г—1.25). Multiplicative penalties preserve zero-at-zero (if raw=0, dampened=0). This follows the precautionary principle: discount unreliable precedent more aggressively than reward confirming precedent.

Layer 2C вҖ” Explainability11. XAI Score SIGNAL

Formula

xai_score = confidence Г— max(shap_shares) where: confidence = SVM regime classification probability (winning class) shap_shares = normalized SHAP feature importance shares [5 features]

What It Measures

Explainability index: how focused and confident the model's reasoning is. High = confident AND one feature dominates. Low = uncertain or diffuse explanation.

Why This Design

Multiplication means both factors must be high for a high score. A confident but diffuse explanation scores low; a focused but uncertain model also scores low. This captures the conjunction: "I know what I'm doing AND I can point to one reason why."

Why It's Correct

Product of two [0,1] values stays in [0,1]. It captures the conjunction property exactly. Feeds legacy TAS at 15% weight.

Layer 4 вҖ” Decision Engine15. Decision Quality Assessment DECISION

Formula

Input Completeness (ic): if agg_conf вүҘ 0.75 AND no unknown_required: ic = "high" elif agg_conf вүҘ 0.55 AND вүӨ1 unknown_required: ic = "medium" else: ic = "low" Signal Agreement (sa): check_1: L3 business_model available? check_2: No insufficient L3 modules? check_3: No high-severity L4 conflicts? sa = "high" (3/3) | "medium" (2/3) | "low" (вүӨ1/3) Assumption Density (ad): h_ratio = heuristic_signals / (heuristic + grounded_signals) ad = "high" (вүҘ0.70) | "medium" (вүҘ0.50) | "low" (otherwise) Overall Uncertainty (continuous blending): mech_contribution = mechanism_uncertainty / 0.30 (maps 0вҖ“0.30 вҶ’ 0вҖ“1) bad_tiers = count(ic=="low") + count(sa=="low") + count(ad=="high") + mech_contribution (continuous sum) if bad_tiers вүҘ 2 OR sarima_stale: overall = "high" elif bad_tiers вүҘ 1: overall = "moderate" else: overall = "low"

What It Measures

Replaces numeric confidence with a structured, multi-axis quality assessment. Each axis measures a different reliability dimension.

Why This Design
  • Three independent axes prevent a single "confidence" number from hiding dimensional failures
  • Mechanism uncertainty blended continuously вҖ” hard-switching on a threshold would lose information
  • The mapping mech_uncertainty / 0.30 normalizes to [0,1] for proportional contribution
Why It's Correct

Continuous blending (discrete + continuous) correctly handles boundary cases. A mechanism_uncertainty of 0.20 adds 0.67 to the count вҖ” not enough alone for "high," but enough when combined with one discrete bad tier. This preserves structural ambiguity when ambiguity is real.
